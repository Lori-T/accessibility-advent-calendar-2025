When using a keyboard, focus should move through the page in a logical order. Users need to understand where they are and what they can do next.

What you're checking today

You're checking that the focus order follows the visual layout and does not skip or trap users.

Quick 5-minute check

  • press Tab to move forwards and Shift + Tab to move backwards
  • check focus moves left to right and top to bottom
  • ensure nothing receives focus unexpectedly
  • confirm you can tab into and out of menus and modals
  • look for keyboard traps where focus cannot escape

The WCAG 2.2 skill behind this

2.4.3 Focus Order - focus must follow a meaningful sequence that preserves relationships.

Why this matters

A confusing focus order disrupts navigation and makes pages unusable for keyboard-only users and assistive tech users.

Try this bonus check

Open a mobile menu. Can you reach all links with the keyboard?
