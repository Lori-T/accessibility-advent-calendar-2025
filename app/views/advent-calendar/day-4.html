Day 4: Check link purpose on the Naughty & Nice List Viewer

The Department of Elfish Affairs has been reviewing the Naughty & Nice List Viewer, a key tool used by Santa’s analysts. Unfortunately, several links simply say Click here or More - which does not tell users what the link actually does.

Today, your task is to check whether each link has a clear purpose from its text alone or from the surrounding context.

Your mission today

Identify links that do not explain their purpose.

Quick 5-minute check

  • link text must describe the destination or action
  • avoid vague labels like here, more, details
  • screen reader users often browse links out of context
  • use descriptive text or aria-label when necessary

Test the Naughty & Nice List Viewer

One link below contains a deliberate accessibility issue.

Review Eve Snowball’s behaviour record ✔

Click here to see more details ❌ (purpose unclear)

What this checks for

2.4.4 link purpose (in context) - users must understand what a link does.

Why this matters

Vague link text creates barriers for screen reader users who browse links in a list without surrounding context.

Bonus challenge

Use your screen reader’s shortcut to list all links. Can you identify the unhelpful one without seeing the page?
