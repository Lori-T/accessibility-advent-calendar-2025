Day 3: Fix headings on the Workshop Log

This morning, the Department of Elfish Affairs received an alert from Santa’s Workshop. The digital Workshop Log - used by elves across multiple teams - has become chaotic. Headings are out of order, skipped entirely, or styled to look like headings without actually being one.

Your task today is to review a snippet of the Workshop Log and check whether the heading structure is logical and accessible.

Your mission today

Make sure headings follow a clear and logical hierarchy.

Quick 5-minute check

  • there should only be one h1 on a page
  • heading levels must not be skipped (for example, from h2 to h4)
  • visual styling should not replace proper HTML headings
  • screen reader users must be able to navigate easily using headings

Test the Workshop Log

Below is an example with one deliberate issue.

Toy production

❌ Production schedule (skips from h2 to h4 - incorrect)

✔ Materials and supplies

What this checks for

1.3.1 info and relationships - structure must be conveyed programmatically.

Why this matters

Users of screen readers navigate by heading. Incorrect structure makes content confusing or impossible to follow.

Bonus challenge

Turn on your browser’s accessibility tree or use a screen reader to navigate by headings only. Can you find the incorrect jump in structure?
