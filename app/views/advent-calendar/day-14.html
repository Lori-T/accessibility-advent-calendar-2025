Some users experience discomfort, dizziness or distraction from animations. Sites should respect the user's reduced motion preference.

What you're checking today

You're checking that animations stop, pause or simplify when the device is set to reduce motion.

Quick 5-minute check

  • turn on Reduce motion in your device settings
  • reload your page
  • check that animations such as scrolling effects, parallax and auto-playing UI animations are reduced
  • ensure nothing essential is lost when motion is reduced

The WCAG 2.2 skill behind this

2.3.3 Animation from Interactions - animations triggered by interactions must be optional.

Why this matters

Reduced motion support helps users avoid discomfort and supports people with vestibular disorders, migraines or sensory sensitivities.

Try this bonus check

Toggle reduced motion off again. Does the behaviour change noticeably?
