The toy factory dashboard looks great on a wide monitor, but the elves noticed that on smaller screens it introduces a horizontal scrollbar and some content becomes hard to read.

Your mission today

Check that content reflows without requiring horizontal scrolling on smaller viewports.

Quick 5-minute check

  • open a page and resize the browser window to a mobile width (around 320px)
  • check whether you need to scroll horizontally to read lines of text
  • look for tables or dashboards that are too wide to fit
  • make sure buttons and labels are not cut off at the edges

Test the toy factory dashboard

Below is a sample dashboard container. It uses a fixed width that can cause horizontal scrolling on small screens.

Toy factory production overview

Today’s production: 12,450 toys - slightly above target.

Watch out for bottlenecks in the painting line and packaging area.

On a small screen or narrow window, this container will force horizontal scrolling. Try narrowing your browser and see what happens.

The WCAG 2.2 skill behind this

1.4.10 reflow - content should reflow on small viewports without requiring users to scroll in two directions.

Why this matters

Many people use services on mobile devices or small screens. Two-dimensional scrolling makes reading and completing tasks much harder, especially for people with motor or visual impairments.

Bonus challenge

Use your browser’s responsive design mode to test a real page on a 320px wide viewport. Do any sections force you to scroll horizontally to read text?
