Day 2: Check colour contrast on the Elves’ Control Panel

Overnight, the Department of Elfish Affairs received a ticket from Santa’s workshop reporting that parts of the Elves’ Control Panel are hard to read. Some elves say the text blends into the background, especially during early-morning shifts when lighting is low.

Today, your task is to review a section of the Control Panel and check whether it meets minimum colour contrast requirements.

Your mission today

Check whether text and interactive elements have enough colour contrast to be readable.

Quick 5-minute check

  • body text must have a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1
  • large text (18px+ or 14px bold) must be at least 3:1
  • text in buttons, links or controls must also meet these ratios
  • placeholder text often fails contrast - check it carefully
  • avoid testing contrast by eye alone - use a contrast checker tool

What this checks for

1.4.3 contrast (minimum) - text must be easy to read against its background.

Why this matters

Poor contrast makes content hard or impossible to read for people with low vision, colour vision deficiencies or when using screens in bright or low light.

Bonus challenge
