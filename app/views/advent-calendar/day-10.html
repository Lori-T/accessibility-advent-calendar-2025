Day 10: Check captions on Santa’s video message

Each December, Santa records a short message for the elves using the Elf Communications Console. But this year’s upload is causing problems - it appears on the dashboard without captions or a transcript.

Today you’ll check whether media content provides accessible alternatives for people who cannot hear the audio.

Your mission today

Check whether Santa’s message includes captions or an accessible transcript.

Quick 5-minute check

  • videos with speech must have captions
  • captions should include both spoken words and meaningful sounds
  • a transcript must be provided if captions are missing
  • auto-generated captions are not always accurate

Review Santa’s message

Below is a message from the Elf Communications Console. One accessibility issue has been deliberately introduced.

Santa’s annual message No captions

❌ Accessibility issue: this video has no captions and no transcript.

What this checks for

1.2.2 Captions (Prerecorded) - prerecorded videos with speech must have captions so that users who cannot hear the audio can still access the content.

Why this matters

Captions make video content accessible to D/deaf and hard of hearing users. They also help people in noisy environments or situations where sound cannot be played.

Bonus challenge

Mute the video or turn your sound off. How much of Santa’s message can you understand?
