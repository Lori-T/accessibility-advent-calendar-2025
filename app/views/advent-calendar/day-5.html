Santa’s reindeer health team has put together a “status report” page, but they forgot to update the page title from a default value.

Your mission today

Check that each page has a clear, unique and descriptive title shown in the browser tab.

Quick 5-minute check

  • open your page in a browser
  • look at the title shown in the browser tab or window
  • check that the title describes the page’s purpose or main content
  • make sure different pages do not all use the same generic title
  • ensure that error pages or confirmation pages also use specific titles

❌ Accessibility issue: the page title in the tab just says “Page”. It should describe this screen, for example “Reindeer health report - Santa HQ”.

The WCAG 2.2 skill behind this

2.4.2 page titled - pages must have titles that describe topic or purpose.

Why this matters

Many users navigate using browser tabs, history or screen reader shortcuts that list page titles. Generic titles like “Home” or “Page” make it hard to find the right content.

Bonus challenge

Open a few pages from your own service and list their titles. Can a user guess the content of each page just from its title?
