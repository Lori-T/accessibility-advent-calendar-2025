Santa’s “naughty or nice meter” shows how well different regions are doing. It looks fun and colourful, but some of the labels are hard to read.

Your mission today

Check that text and essential icons have enough contrast against their background.

Quick 5-minute check

  • pick a page with coloured panels, tags or charts
  • identify text that sits on coloured backgrounds
  • use a colour contrast checker to test text against its background
  • make sure normal text meets at least a 4.5:1 contrast ratio
  • check small icons or symbols if they convey meaning

The WCAG 2.2 skill behind this

1.4.3 contrast (minimum) - text and images of text must have enough contrast with their background.

Why this matters

Low contrast text is hard to read for many people, especially in bright light or on lower quality screens. It particularly affects users with low vision or colour vision deficiency.

Bonus challenge

Use a colour picker to sample the text and background colours from the “borderline” panel. Put the values into a contrast checker - does it pass?
