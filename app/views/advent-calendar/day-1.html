Day 2: Check colour contrast on the Elves’ control panel

As part of your ongoing work with the Department of Elfish Affairs (DEA), you have been asked to review part of the Elves’ control panel. Some elves have reported that parts of the panel are difficult to read during long shifts in the workshop.

Today, you are checking whether text and controls on the panel have enough contrast to be readable for everyone, including people with low vision or colour vision differences.

Your mission today

Check that text and interactive elements have sufficient colour contrast.

Quick 5-minute check

  • body text must have a contrast ratio of at least 4.5 to 1
  • large text must have a contrast ratio of at least 3 to 1
  • buttons, links and form controls must also meet contrast requirements
  • do not rely on colour alone to communicate important information
  • use a contrast checker rather than judging by eye

Examine the Elves’ control panel

Below is a simplified section of the control panel. One example has a deliberate colour contrast issue.

Workshop status

❌ This text is too light against the background and does not meet minimum contrast requirements.

✔ This text has sufficient contrast and is easy to read.

What this checks for

1.4.3 contrast (minimum) - text and controls must be readable against their background.

Why this matters

Poor colour contrast can make content difficult or impossible to read for people with low vision, colour vision deficiencies, or when using screens in bright or low light conditions.

Finding this issue using the Workshop Accessibility Verification Engine (WAVE)

When WAVE is run on a page like this, it flags text and controls that may not meet colour contrast requirements.

WAVE can quickly highlight potential contrast failures, but you should always confirm results using a colour contrast checker and consider the context of the content.

Bonus challenge

Use a contrast checker to test the failing examples. What colour changes would make them pass?
