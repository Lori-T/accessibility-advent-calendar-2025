The elves at Santa HQ have launched a new “North Pole photo feed” that shows images captured during test flights. Before it goes live, they need your help checking whether the images are accessible to everyone — including people who rely on screen readers.

Your mission today

Check that every meaningful image has an appropriate text alternative.

Quick 5-minute check

  • look at each image and decide whether it is meaningful or decorative
  • for meaningful images, check they have clear, concise alt text
  • for decorative images, check they use alt=""
  • make sure any text shown inside an image appears as real text elsewhere
  • icons used as links or buttons must have an accessible name

The WCAG 2.2 skill behind this

1.1.1 non-text content – meaningful images must have accessible alternatives.

Why this matters

Without alt text, people who are blind or have very low vision miss essential information.

Bonus challenge

Open your browser’s accessibility tree or use a screen reader. Which images are announced clearly, and which cause confusion?
