Santa’s logistics team share updates through a dashboard of important links. Unfortunately, several of the links just say “click here”, which is not very helpful.

Your mission today

Check that link text makes sense out of context and clearly describes the destination or action.

Quick 5-minute check

  • scan the page for links that say things like “click here” or “more”
  • check whether the link text alone explains what will happen
  • if it does not, consider rewriting the link text
  • avoid repeating the same link text for different destinations

Test the Santa logistics links

Below is a list of links used by Santa’s logistics team. There is a deliberate accessibility issue: several links use the same vague wording.

  • Reindeer flight schedule - {# ❌ Deliberate issue: vague link text #} click here
  • Toy factory maintenance log - click here
  • View weather conditions for Christmas Eve - view
  • Check toy quality control results - more

Imagine using a screen reader shortcut that lists links out of context - “click here”, “click here”, “view”, “more”. It is not clear which link does what.

The WCAG 2.2 skill behind this

2.4.4 link purpose (in context) - the purpose of each link should be clear from its text, or from its text together with nearby context.

Why this matters

Clear link text helps everyone scan a page quickly. It is especially important for people who use screen readers or voice input, and people with some cognitive impairments.

Bonus challenge

On one of your own pages, copy and paste just the links into a list. Would they still make sense without the surrounding paragraphs?
