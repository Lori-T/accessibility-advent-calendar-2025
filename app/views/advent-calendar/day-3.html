Page titles help users understand where they are. They appear in browser tabs, bookmarks and search results, and are the first thing screen readers announce.

What you’re checking today

You’re checking whether your page has a clear, descriptive title that accurately reflects the content.

Quick 5-minute check

  • look at the text shown in the browser tab
  • right-click and select Inspect to find the <title> element in the <head>
  • ensure the title is unique, descriptive and follows the pattern “Page name – Service name”
  • check that the title makes sense even when viewed out of context

The WCAG 2.2 skill behind this

2.4.2 Page Titled - pages must have meaningful and descriptive titles.

Why this matters

Informative titles help all users, especially those using screen readers or navigating many open tabs.

Try this bonus check

Open your page in a private browser window. Does the title still make perfect sense?
