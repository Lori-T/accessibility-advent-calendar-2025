Headings act as signposts. They help users - including screen reader users - understand how your content is organised.

What you’re checking today

You’re checking whether your headings form a logical and meaningful outline.

Quick 5-minute check

  • right-click and select Inspect
  • search for <h1, <h2, and <h3 using Ctrl + F
  • ensure there is only one <h1> per page
  • make sure heading levels are not skipped (for example, h1 → h3)
  • check headings describe the content that follows

The WCAG 2.2 skill behind this

1.3.1 Info and Relationships - headings must be marked up correctly to convey structure.

Why this matters

Good structure helps people navigate, skim content and understand relationships between sections.

Try this bonus check

Use your screen reader’s “headings list” command. Does your content outline make sense?
