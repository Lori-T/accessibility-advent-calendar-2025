Clear, simple language makes content easier to understand for everyone. It is especially important for users with cognitive impairments, people reading in a second language and those under time pressure.

What you're checking today

You're checking for jargon, complex words, unexplained acronyms and sentences that are too long or complicated.

Quick 5-minute check

  • choose a paragraph of content and read it out loud
  • highlight any jargon or unfamiliar terms
  • look for long sentences with multiple clauses
  • break complex information into shorter sentences or lists
  • use tools like Hemingway Editor to identify readability issues

The WCAG 2.2 skill behind this

3.1.5 Reading Level - content should not require a reading level that is too advanced unless essential.

Why this matters

Plain language helps people understand information more quickly and reduces cognitive load. It makes your content more inclusive for everyone.

Try this bonus check

Ask yourself: would someone unfamiliar with this service understand it the first time they read it?
